\aufgabe{Minimum Expected Cost Principle}{

    Given a cost matrix $\mathbf{C}$
    \begin{center}
        \begin{tabular}{c c | c c}
            \multirow{2}{*}{} & \multirow{2}{*}{} & \multicolumn{2}{c}{True class} \\
                & & $y=1$ & $y = -1$ \\
            \hline
            Predicted & $\yh = 1$ & $C(1, 1) = 0$ & $C(1, -1) = 1$ \\
            class    & $\yh = -1$ & $C(-1, 1) = \frac{n_{-}}{n_{+}}$ & $C(-1, -1) = 0$ \\
        \end{tabular}
    \end{center}
    where $n_{+}, n_{-}$ denote the number of testing samples with label $1$ and $-1$, respectively. We further assume that we have knowledge of $p(\cdot | \xv)$. According to the Minimum Expected Cost Principle, $\xv$ as class $1$ if 
    \begin{align*}
        \E_{K\sim p(\cdot | \xv)} (C(1, K)) \leq \E_{K\sim p(\cdot | \xv)} (C(-1, K)).
    \end{align*}
    This yields the optimal threshold $c^*$ for the probabilitic classifier $h(\xv) = 2 \cdot \mathbbm{1}_{[\pi(\xv) \geq c^*]} - 1$. If we append more samples of class $-1$ to the test set and recompute the optimal threshold for $h(\xv)$, how will PPV and TPR change?
    
}
